According to the latest board memo, after-school programs will see reduced funding due to budget cuts next year. A revised budget proposal will go to the school board for a vote.

The revised proposal reportedly softens the original cut slightly by drawing down a portion of the district's reserve fund, a move some board members have criticized as a short-term fix that just delays a harder conversation to the following year. Supporters of the revised proposal argue that giving programs a smaller, more gradual cut this year gives schools more time to find alternative funding sources like grants or community partnerships. The vote is expected to be close, based on informal comments a few board members have already made publicly ahead of the meeting.
