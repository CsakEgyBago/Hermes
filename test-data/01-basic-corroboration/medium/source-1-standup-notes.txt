During yesterday's incident, checkout errors were traced to the payment worker running out of memory. The on-call engineer restarted the payment worker as a first response. Customers affected were offered a ten percent discount as an apology. The rollback script had to be run twice before the affected pods stabilized. Standup this morning focused entirely on the postmortem timeline. A follow-up ticket was created to add memory limits to the payment worker deployment.

The team spent a good chunk of standup debating whether the restart alone would have been sufficient without the rollback, since the two happened close enough together that it's genuinely unclear which one resolved the underlying issue. Someone raised the idea of adding a canary deployment step for the payment worker specifically, given how central it is to checkout. There was general agreement that the current alerting threshold for memory usage on that service is set too high to catch this kind of gradual leak early. Notes from the discussion were added to the shared incident doc, and the follow-up ticket was assigned a priority tag ahead of next sprint planning.
