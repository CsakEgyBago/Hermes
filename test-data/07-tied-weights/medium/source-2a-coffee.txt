The break room coffee machine was replaced in June. The old one had been due for retirement for months.

The old machine had been the subject of an ongoing office joke for at least a year, with a running tally of how many times a week someone had to jiggle the handle just to get it to start brewing. Facilities finally approved a replacement after the machine started leaking onto the counter with some regularity, which pushed it from an annoyance to an actual maintenance concern. The new model was chosen partly based on an informal office poll about brew strength preferences, which facilities admits probably wasn't the most rigorous selection process but seemed to make people happy.
