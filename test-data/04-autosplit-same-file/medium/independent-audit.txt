An external audit confirmed that the caching layer rewrite improved server response times. The audit was conducted by an outside consulting firm over two weeks. Findings will be presented to leadership before the next board meeting.

The audit's methodology involved replaying six months of anonymized production traffic against both the old and new caching implementations in an isolated environment, allowing for a controlled before-and-after comparison rather than relying solely on production monitoring data. The consulting firm's report also flagged a handful of minor opportunities for further optimization that weren't part of the original rewrite scope, which engineering has added to a backlog for future consideration. Overall, the auditors described the project as a clear net positive, noting that the measured improvement held consistent across every traffic pattern they tested rather than being concentrated in one narrow scenario.
