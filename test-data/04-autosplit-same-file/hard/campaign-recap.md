# Marketing Recap

The Q3 campaign drove a twelve percent increase in signups.

Paid social spend was the single largest driver of the increase. Email open rates also ticked up slightly during the campaign window. The creative team ran four distinct ad variants throughout the quarter, and the two that leaned on customer testimonials rather than product screenshots consistently outperformed the others by a wide margin. Budget was reallocated toward those two variants roughly halfway through the campaign once the early performance gap became clear, which likely accounts for some of the acceleration in signups seen in the back half of the quarter.

# Design Recap

The Q3 campaign drove a twelve percent increase in signups.

The refreshed landing page layout tested well in early user interviews. A/B testing will continue into the following campaign cycle. The design team also simplified the signup form itself, cutting it from six fields down to three, which internal analytics suggest may have contributed meaningfully to the improved conversion rate independent of the ad creative changes. A follow-up study is planned to try to separate the form simplification's impact from the ad campaign's impact, since right now the two effects are somewhat entangled in the topline number.
