The school district announced budget cuts affecting after-school programs next year. Parent groups have started a petition. The district cited a shortfall in state funding as the main driver.

The petition has gathered several thousand signatures within its first week, and organizers plan to present it formally at the next school board meeting. District officials have said the cuts are proportional across all schools rather than targeting any one campus specifically, though some parents have questioned whether that approach fairly accounts for schools that rely more heavily on after-school programs for working families. A separate fundraising effort led by a parent-teacher association at one school has already raised enough to partially offset cuts at that campus specifically.
