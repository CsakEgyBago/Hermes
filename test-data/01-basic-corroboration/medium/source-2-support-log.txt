Support tickets mentioned checkout failures, which the engineering team linked to the payment worker running out of memory. A few customers reported being double-charged, and refunds are being processed manually today. The incident review is scheduled for next Tuesday. Ticket volume peaked around 11 AM local time before tapering off. One enterprise customer asked for a formal incident report by end of day. The support team used a canned response until an official statement was ready.

Several agents noted that customers were understanding once they heard a fix was already in progress, and very few tickets required more than one follow-up message. The double-charge cases are being tracked in a separate spreadsheet so finance can reconcile them against the payment processor's own records once those are available. A couple of tickets turned out to be unrelated password-reset requests that just happened to arrive during the same window, which briefly inflated the apparent scope of the incident before anyone noticed the mismatch. The support lead plans to bring a short summary of ticket themes to the Tuesday review, separate from the engineering-side postmortem.
