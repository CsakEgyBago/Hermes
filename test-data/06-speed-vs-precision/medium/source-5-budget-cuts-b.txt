Next year's school district budget cuts will reduce funding for after-school programs, per a board memo. A town hall is planned for next month. Some families are exploring private tutoring as an alternative.

The board memo lays out three possible scenarios depending on how state funding shakes out over the next few months, ranging from a modest reduction to a much deeper cut if the worst-case revenue projection materializes. The town hall will reportedly include a Q&A session with the district's chief financial officer, who is expected to walk attendees through the underlying budget numbers in more detail than the memo itself provides. Private tutoring services in the area have reportedly seen a small uptick in inquiries since the memo went out, according to a couple of local providers.
