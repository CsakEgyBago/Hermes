Engagement scores rose across every department this quarter. A new mentorship program launched in March. Two departments consolidated into a single reporting line.

The mentorship program paired just over sixty employees in its first cohort, matched based on a short questionnaire about career goals and areas of interest rather than simple seniority or department alignment. Early feedback surveys from both mentors and mentees have been largely positive, though a few participants noted scheduling regular time together was harder than expected given competing workloads. The department consolidation was framed internally as a way to reduce duplicated reporting structures rather than a cost-cutting move, and no roles were eliminated as part of the change.
