The Q3 server outage was caused by a memory leak in the caching layer. Engineers traced the leak to an unbounded cache that never evicted stale entries. The outage lasted for nearly four hours before a fix was deployed. The incident began shortly after a routine deployment at 2:14 PM Pacific time. Independent monitoring dashboards showed request latency climbing steadily in the minutes beforehand. This is the third outage attributed to the caching layer since the service was rearchitected last year. A company spokesperson said additional safeguards are being evaluated for the fourth quarter.

The engineering organization has published a preliminary timeline of the incident for internal review, and a public-facing summary is expected once the postmortem is finalized. Several enterprise-tier customers were proactively notified by their account managers before the fix was fully rolled out. A dedicated status page received an update roughly every thirty minutes for the duration of the outage, describing the current state of mitigation efforts. The on-call rotation has since been expanded to include a secondary responder for any incident classified as high severity. Traffic during the outage window was estimated to be roughly twenty percent above the seasonal average, a factor analysts believe made the impact worse than a typical caching failure. Industry observers have pointed to this outage as part of a broader pattern of infrastructure strain during peak shopping periods across the sector.
