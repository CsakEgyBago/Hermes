Marketing campaign assets needed to be revised for the new date after the launch was delayed by one week due to a critical security bug found during final testing. The social media rollout plan was also pushed back accordingly. Paid ad spend for the original launch date was paused to avoid wasted impressions. The creative team is reworking hero banner copy to reference the new date. Press embargo lift time was pushed back to match the new schedule.

The marketing team's update notes that pausing paid spend for even a few days required manual intervention across three different ad platforms, since none of them support a clean one-click pause-and-resume for scheduled campaigns. The creative rework is mostly limited to date references and a couple of screenshots that showed a now-outdated version of the product UI; the core messaging and value proposition are staying the same. Several partner publications that had agreed to cover the launch under embargo were individually contacted to confirm they could still honor the new lift time, and all of them confirmed without issue. The team is treating the extra week as a small silver lining, since it gives the design team more runway to polish a few last-minute visual details on the landing page.
