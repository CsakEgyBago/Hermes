A critical security bug was discovered during final testing, which pushed the product launch back by one week. Beta testers had flagged a related but distinct performance issue two weeks earlier that was already fixed. The vulnerability allowed unauthorized access to draft documents. The disclosure was handled under the company's standard responsible-disclosure process. A patch was deployed to all affected environments within six hours of discovery. An external auditor has been engaged to review the fix independently.

The security report classifies the vulnerability as high severity but not critical, on the basis that exploiting it required an authenticated session and knowledge of a predictable internal identifier rather than being exploitable anonymously. No evidence was found in the access logs suggesting the vulnerability had actually been exploited before it was patched, though log retention only covers the preceding thirty days. The earlier performance issue flagged by beta testers is explicitly called out as unrelated in cause, despite surfacing in the same subsystem, to avoid the two being conflated in future audits. The external auditor's engagement letter specifies a two-week review window, with a public summary of findings expected to follow once the review concludes.
