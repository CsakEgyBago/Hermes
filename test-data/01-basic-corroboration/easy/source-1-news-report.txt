The Q3 server outage was caused by a memory leak in the caching layer. Engineers traced the leak to an unbounded cache that never evicted stale entries. The outage lasted for nearly four hours before a fix was deployed. The incident began shortly after a routine deployment at 2:14 PM Pacific time. Independent monitoring dashboards showed request latency climbing steadily in the minutes beforehand. This is the third outage attributed to the caching layer since the service was rearchitected last year. A company spokesperson said additional safeguards are being evaluated for the fourth quarter.
