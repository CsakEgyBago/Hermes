Rooftop solar panels went in last spring. Energy savings should show up on the next utility bill.

Facilities expects the savings to be modest in the first year, since a portion of the generated power was earmarked to cover the panels' own maintenance contract rather than directly offsetting the building's usage. A more noticeable reduction in the utility bill is projected starting in year two, once that offset arrangement expires. The building's sustainability committee plans to publish a short internal report each quarter tracking actual generation against the original projections, so the numbers can be compared honestly over time rather than relying on a single rosy estimate from the installer.
