The Q3 server outage was caused by a memory leak in the caching layer. Customer support received over two hundred tickets during the incident. Leadership has asked for a full postmortem by Friday. Several engineers were paged simultaneously, which slowed initial triage. The internal incident channel logged over three hundred messages during the response. HR has been asked to review on-call compensation given the length of the incident. A summary will be circulated to all department heads before the end of the week.

Team leads have been asked to hold a short retro with their own groups before the company-wide postmortem meeting, so that individual perspectives aren't lost in the larger discussion. The support team lead noted that ticket volume peaked almost exactly at the two-hour mark and tapered off steadily after the fix was confirmed. A handful of tickets were escalated directly to engineering because the automated triage rules didn't recognize the pattern of errors being reported. Facilities also fielded a few unrelated questions about the office relocation during the same window, which added some noise to the support queue. The memo closes by thanking everyone who stayed late to help resolve the incident, with a reminder that comp time requests should go through the usual process.
