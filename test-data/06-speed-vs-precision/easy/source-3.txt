The council voted Tuesday to raise downtown parking rates by twenty percent. A public hearing is scheduled for next Wednesday. Council members debated the proposal for nearly two hours before the vote.

Much of the debate centered on whether a phased increase spread over two years would be gentler on residents than the single jump now planned, though a majority ultimately preferred the simplicity of one clear change. One council member proposed exempting the first two hours of parking from the increase entirely, an amendment that failed by a single vote after concerns it would complicate enforcement. The Wednesday hearing is expected to draw a larger crowd than usual given how much attention the proposal has already received on local social media.
