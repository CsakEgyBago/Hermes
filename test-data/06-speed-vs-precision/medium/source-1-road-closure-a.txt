The city council approved a six-month closure of Main Street for bridge repairs. Local businesses are worried about lost foot traffic. Signage guiding drivers around the closure went up over the weekend.

The bridge itself has been flagged as structurally deficient in two consecutive annual inspections, which is why the council ultimately prioritized the repair despite the disruption it will cause downtown. A small business relief fund is being discussed as a way to offset some of the expected revenue loss, though no details have been finalized yet. City engineers estimate the repair could theoretically be done faster with a full weekend closure instead, but that approach was rejected due to safety concerns about compressed construction schedules.
