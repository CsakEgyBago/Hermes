Root cause: the payment worker ran out of memory, causing checkout errors during the incident window. Customers affected were offered a ten percent discount as an apology. Monitoring alerts were delayed by several minutes due to a dashboard outage. The five whys analysis pointed to a recent change in batch job scheduling as a contributing factor. A follow-up action item assigns memory profiling to the platform team. The postmortem doc was shared with the broader engineering org for visibility.

The timeline section of the postmortem breaks the incident into four phases: onset, detection, mitigation, and recovery, with detection identified as the phase that took longest relative to the others. Reviewers flagged the delayed monitoring alert as a separate, lower-priority issue worth its own investigation rather than folding it into this incident's root cause. Two additional action items were added during the review meeting: one to add a staging-environment load test that mimics the batch job schedule change, and another to document the payment worker's expected memory ceiling somewhere more discoverable than a single engineer's notes. The postmortem was marked blameless in its header, consistent with the team's usual practice for this kind of review.
