Customer support was told the launch was delayed by one week due to a critical security bug found during final testing. The beta tester group was expanded to catch similar bugs earlier next time. No customer data was affected by the bug. Support macros were updated so agents give a consistent answer about the delay. A handful of enterprise accounts asked for an extended trial period as compensation. Escalations related to the delay dropped off within two days.

The support brief includes a short script agents can use if a customer asks for technical detail beyond what the public statement covers, which mostly redirects them to the upcoming security report rather than speculating. Of the enterprise accounts that asked for an extended trial, all but one were approved automatically under an existing policy for launch-related delays; the remaining account is being handled individually because their contract has non-standard terms. Support leadership noted that having the FAQ ready before the delay was publicly announced made a noticeable difference in how smoothly the first day of inquiries went. A short survey will go out to a sample of affected customers next month to gauge whether the delay affected their overall satisfaction.
